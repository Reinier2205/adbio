HOW TO USE

First time setup

1
OPEN ON BOTH PHONES
Open this page on both phones — the caravan phone and the driver's phone. No app download needed. Share the link via AirDrop, WhatsApp or iMessage before you arrive at the campsite.
2
PLACE THE SENSOR PHONE
Put the caravan phone flat inside the caravan — on a table, bench or floor. Point the top of the phone toward the hitch end (where it connects to the car). This ensures pitch and roll read correctly. Make sure it won't slide around.
3
TAP SENSOR
On the caravan phone, tap SENSOR. On iOS, tap Allow Motion when prompted. A 4-digit PIN appears at the top — this is your permanent pairing code.
4
CONNECT THE DISPLAY
On the driver's phone, tap DISPLAY and type the 4-digit PIN. Connection happens automatically on the 4th digit. You can also tap 📤 SHARE on the sensor to send a link that connects instantly.
5
LEVEL THE CARAVAN
The display shows a top-down caravan. The dot and brake lights show your status:

🟢 Green — level (less than 1°)
🟠 Orange — close (1° – 3°)
🔴 Red — needs adjusting (more than 3°)

Left / Right (Roll): Drive onto leveling blocks under the low wheels until the dot centres left-right.

Front / Back (Pitch): Use the jockey wheel to raise or lower the hitch until the dot centres front-to-back.

The dashed circle on the caravan marks the 3° boundary — when the dot is inside the circle you're in the orange or green zone.

Once connected, a row of buttons appears at the bottom of both screens: TALK · MUTE · CAM · END — for voice calls and live camera. See the 🎙️ VOICE & CAM tab for details.

Next time you use it

🔗 AUTOMATIC RECONNECT
Both phones remember each other after the first pairing. Next time, just open the app and tap SENSOR or DISPLAY — they reconnect automatically using the saved PIN. No typing needed.
📺 SCREEN STAYS ON
Both phones keep their screens on automatically while leveling — no need to touch the screen to prevent it going dark. If the screen does go off, just wake it up and the connection resumes automatically.
🔄 RECONNECT
If a phone gets stuck connecting, a RECONNECT button appears after 8 seconds. Tap it to retry using the saved PIN. No need to go back to the home screen. If the display opens before the sensor is ready, it will automatically keep retrying every few seconds.

Solo mode

🫧 ONE PHONE ONLY
Tap SOLO MODE on the home screen to use just one phone inside the caravan. You'll see the live tilt and guidance directly on that phone. Solo mode never saves pairing state — it always starts fresh.

Pairing management

⚠️ PHONE PLACEMENT
The sensor phone must be completely flat and not move while leveling. Even a small tilt in the phone's resting position will offset all readings.
PAIRED WITH
This name is saved on this phone only — helps you remember which device you're paired with.

Voice call

1
START A CALL
Once both phones are connected and showing the leveling screen, a button row appears at the bottom: TALK · MUTE · CAM · END.

Tap TALK on either phone to start a voice call. The other phone shows ANSWER — tap it to connect. Both phones will hear each other through the speaker.
2
MUTE
Tap MUTE on either phone to silence both sides at once — useful when the phones are close together to prevent echo, or when you need quiet. Tap again to unmute both phones together.
3
END THE CALL
Tap END to hang up. The leveling display stays active — you can start a new call any time by tapping TALK again.
📢 ECHO & FEEDBACK
If both phones are near each other when you first connect, you may hear echo. Tap MUTE immediately — this mutes both phones. Once the sensor phone is inside the caravan and the driver is outside, unmute and the echo will be gone.

Live camera

1
ENABLE THE CAMERA
Tap CAM on either phone to activate the live camera feed. The sensor phone's rear camera turns on and broadcasts to the driver's phone.

— The Sensor phone shows a full-screen preview of what its camera sees — useful for aiming at the hitch.
— The Display phone shows the live feed full-screen, so the driver can see the hitch area while reversing or adjusting.
2
SWITCH BACK TO LEVELING
Tap CAM OFF (the same button, now amber) on either phone to turn off the camera and return to the leveling view on both screens. Voice stays active in the background.
📍 SENSOR PHONE PLACEMENT FOR CAMERA
When using the camera, prop the sensor phone so its rear camera faces the hitch. A small stand, a cup, or leaning it against something works well. The leveling data is still being sent even while the camera is on.
🔋 BATTERY TIP
The camera uses more battery than leveling alone. Turn it off with CAM OFF once you've finished reversing — the leveling view uses much less power for the fine-adjustment phase.
🔊 VOICE + CAMERA TOGETHER
Voice and camera work independently. You can have a live call active while the camera is off, or turn the camera on mid-call. The MUTE and END buttons always control the voice call regardless of camera state.

How it connects

LevelVan uses WebRTC — a direct peer-to-peer connection between the two phones. Tilt data, voice calls, and live camera all flow directly between devices with no server in the middle after the initial handshake.
📶 Same WiFi network
Both phones on the same home or campsite WiFi. This is the easiest setup — everything works automatically. The initial handshake uses the PeerJS relay server briefly, then data flows directly over your local network.
🌐 Internet available (mobile data)
If both phones have mobile data (even on different networks), the WebRTC handshake completes via the internet. Works well in most campgrounds with any signal.
🏔️ No signal — remote location
No WiFi, no mobile data? Use a phone hotspot:
  1. On the Display phone, turn on a Personal Hotspot.
  2. On the Sensor phone, connect to that hotspot via WiFi settings.
  3. Open LevelVan on both and connect as normal.
  4. The display phone can create the hotspot and run the display simultaneously.

The hotspot phone often has enough signal to complete the initial handshake even when the other phone doesn't.

Technical details

🔢 PERMANENT PIN
The sensor generates a 4-digit PIN on first use and saves it. Both phones remember this PIN permanently. Tapping SENSOR or DISPLAY always uses the saved PIN — no re-entry needed. Only RESET PAIRING clears it.
📐 SENSOR DATA
Uses the DeviceOrientation API — gamma for roll (left/right) and beta for pitch (front/back). iOS requires explicit permission. Data is sent at ~15Hz (every 66ms). Green = <1°, Orange = 1–3°, Red = >3°.
💡 BUBBLE SCALE
The dot travels the full width of the caravan body between 0° and 5°. The dashed circle marks exactly 3° — when the dot is inside the circle you're within 3° of level. This gives fine visual feedback in the range that matters most.
📺 MULTIPLE DISPLAYS
Any number of phones can connect to the same sensor at once using the same PIN. All displays receive the same live data stream simultaneously.
⚡ RELAY SERVER
The initial WebRTC handshake uses 0.peerjs.com (free public server) for a few seconds to exchange connection details. After that, all data is peer-to-peer. The relay server never sees your tilt data, voice, or video.
🎙️ VOICE & VIDEO CHANNELS
Voice uses a dedicated WebRTC audio channel separate from the tilt data channel. Camera uses a third WebRTC media channel. All three run simultaneously over the same P2P connection. STUN servers (Google's public infrastructure) are used to establish connections across different mobile networks and hotspots.
📹 VIDEO SPECS
Camera streams at 640×480, 20fps with the motion content hint — optimised for low latency over mobile data rather than image quality. This keeps latency under 200ms on a typical 4G connection.
