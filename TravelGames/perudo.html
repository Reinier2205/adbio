PERUDO

The Ancient Game of Liar's Dice

The Objective

Be the last player with at least one die remaining. Perudo is a game of bluffing, probability, and nerves of steel. You don't need to have the best dice; you just need your friends to think you do.

The Setup

  • Each player starts with 5 dice and a cup.
  • Everyone shakes their cup and flips it onto the table, keeping the dice hidden from others.
  • You may look at your own dice at any time, but keep them secret!

How to Play

The first player makes a Bid. A bid consists of a Quantity and a Face Value (e.g., "Four 5s").

The next player must then choose one of two actions:

1. Raise the Bid

You must increase the previous bid. You can:

  • Increase the Quantity (e.g., "Five 5s").
  • Increase the Face Value (e.g., "Four 6s").
  • Increase Both.

2. Call "Dudo" (Liar)

If you think the current bid is impossible, shout "Dudo!". Everyone reveals their dice.

  • If the bid was wrong (fewer dice than claimed), the bidder loses a die.
  • If the bid was right (equal or more), the challenger loses a die.

The Wild Card: Aces 1

In Perudo, the 1 (Aces) are Wild. They count as whatever face value is currently being bid.

Example: If the bid is "Five 4s," and there are three 4s and two 1s on the table, the bid is successful!

Bidding Ladder

Bid Type Example Rule
Normal Raise Three 4s → Four 4s Higher quantity, same value.
Value Raise Three 4s → Three 5s Same quantity, higher value.
Jumping to Aces Six 4s → Three 1s You can bid Aces by halving the current quantity.
Leaving Aces Two 1s → Five 2s Double the Aces plus one to return to normal numbers.

Calza (Optional Rule)

If you believe the bid is exactly correct, you can shout "Calza!". If you are right, you regain a previously lost die. If you are wrong, you lose one.
