Perudo

The Guide to Pirate's Dice

The Objective

Perudo is a game of deception and probability. Each player starts with 5 dice. Your goal is to be the last player with dice remaining. You lose dice when your bluff is called, or when you incorrectly call someone else a liar.

The Wild Aces

The Ace (1) is always Wild. It counts as any number currently being bid. If there are four 6s and two Aces on the table, there are actually six 6s in play.

Table Rules

Action Condition
Raise Count Increase the quantity (e.g., 3 Fours → 4 Fours)
Raise Value Keep count, increase face (e.g., 3 Fours → 3 Fives)
Call Dudo Challenge the previous player. Count all dice + Aces!

Secret Strategies

Master these three concepts to dominate the table:

The Rule of Three

Statistically, any number (including Aces) will appear once for every 3 dice on the table. If there are 12 dice total, a bid of "Four" is statistically safe.

The "Phantom" Bid

Early in the round, bid a number you don't have. This tricks opponents into thinking you are holding that value, leading them into a trap later.

The Palafico Round

When you have only 1 die left, you are Palafico. In this special round, Aces are not wild. Use this to catch out cocky players!

Aggressive Aces

Jumping to Aces (halving the bid) is a great way to "reset" a high-stakes bidding war and put the pressure back on the first player.
