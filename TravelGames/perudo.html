Perudo

The Art of the Pirate's Bluff

Objective

Be the last player with dice. You start with 5 dice. When a player is challenged (Dudo) and loses, they lose one die from their cup. Once you're out of dice, you're out of the game.

Aces are Wild

The 1 (Ace) is the wild card of Perudo. In every normal round, Aces count as whatever number is currently being bid.

Example: If the current bid is "Six 4s", you count every 4 AND every Ace on the table.

Making a Bid

To Raise Action
The Quantity Keep the face value (e.g. 5) but increase the count (from 3 to 4).
The Face Value Keep the count (e.g. 3) but increase the face value (from 5 to 6).
Call Dudo If you think the total count (Value + Aces) is lower than the bid.

Secret Strategy Tips

For players who want to win, not just play:

The Rule of Three

On average, any specific number (including Aces) will appear once for every 3 dice on the table. Use this to spot impossible bids.

The Ace Reset

If the bid is getting dangerously high (e.g., 8 Fives), you can "halve" the bid to switch to Aces (e.g., 4 Aces). It resets the pressure.

The False Lead

Bid a number you don't have early in the game. It makes players think you're holding those dice, allowing you to trap them later.
