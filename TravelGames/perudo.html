The Wild Aces

The

Aces (1s) are the most powerful dice in the game.

They are Wild. This means they count as whatever face value is currently being bid. If the bid is "Seven 6s," every Ace on the table is added to the total count of 6s.

The Rules of the Table

Your Move Requirement
Raise Quantity Keep the die value, but increase the number (e.g., 4 Fives → 5 Fives).
Raise Value Keep the quantity, but increase the die face (e.g., 4 Fives → 4 Sixes).

Elite Strategy

Inside info for the discerning player:

The "Aces" Pivot

When the bidding gets too high (e.g., "Nine 6s"), jump to Aces. You only need to bid half the current quantity (e.g., "Five Aces") to reset the tension.

Probability Play

Always remember: The total number of dice divided by 3 is the "Safe Zone" for any bid. Anything higher is a pure bluff.

The Reverse Bluff

Bidding a number you actually have plenty of is sometimes too obvious. Try bidding a number you have zero of early on to lead others into a trap.

The End-Game

When you are down to 1 die, you are Palafico. In this round, Aces are not wild. Use this to catch opponents who forget the change in rules!
